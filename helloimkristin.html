Opportunity:  Many musicians struggle to profit off their work and many music fans are seeking ways to discover and support musicians. Project Presentation Report
Process & Deliverables: Below are deliverables you go through to read more in-depth about the various UX methods my team & I conducted throughout the project.
Investigate To start, I begin my UX Design journey by defining who users needs and brand goals. Further, I analyze the competitive and comparative markets to learn as much as possible from what has been done (specifically noting what works & what doesn’t). + Quantitative surveys + User interviews + Stakeholder interviews + Contextual inquiry + Brand analysis + Market analysis (competitive + comparative)
ACTUALIZE & TEST & EVOLVE Once the project scope is refined, I jump into the creation stage. This is my favorite part, where I shine & have the most fun. At this point I’ve finally made sense of the mess & the project focus is now more clear. Typically, I will go through several validated design cycles involving heavy sketching, prototyping, usability testing, & iterations. + User flows + Sitemaps & appmaps + Design studio + Sketching + Wireframes (paper, lo-fidelity, med-fidelity, hi-fidelity) + Prototyping (paper, Axure, Invision) + High-fidelity mockups with annotations

TOOL KIT
As the backbone of my process, I follow a loose IDEATE UX flow. Why loose? Projects are rarely alike, so my process adapts to the circumstances. A common phrase in User Experience Design is “It depends…”, meaning certain User Experience Design methods can be more or less appropriate in specific instances. I’ve been formally trained with a Lean UX toolkit which keeps me prepares for any project scope. And the learning never stops; I continually look towards “Next Steps”, seeking out new tips & tricks of the trade from mentors to help me iterate my own UX practices. 
Decipher & Empathize Once I gather enough information, I synthesize all of the research by organizing all the information into patterns based on behaviors, context, pain points, & pleasure points of users and the market. Once patterns are identified, I can refine the goals & establish guidelines for the MVP (minimum viable product) of the project + Affinity diagraming + Feature prioritization + MoSCoW method. + Personas + User stories & journeys + Card sorting
